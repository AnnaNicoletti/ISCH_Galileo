Project Description

The project ‚ÄúGalileo ‚Äì Between Science and Cultural Memory‚Äù was developed as part of the course Information Science and Cultural Heritage (Academic Year 2024‚Äì2025). Its main goal is to semantically represent the figure of Galileo Galilei by using standards and practices of Linked Open Data for Libraries, Archives and Museums (LODLAM). Starting from a curated selection of ten heterogeneous items ‚Äì including works, historical figures, events, and cultural objects, as well as one full-text document ‚Äì the project builds a consistent and interoperable conceptual model to make these resources accessible, linkable, and meaningful within the Semantic Web. This work embraces a multidisciplinary approach, combining historical research, metadata modeling, and digital encoding, in order to reveal the complexity of Galileo‚Äôs identity ‚Äì not just as a scientist, but also as a symbol of modernity and cultural transformation.

Complete Conceptual Graph

The diagram below represents the complete conceptual graph of the project, showing how all ten items are semantically connected to each other.
It was created using the Graffoo notation (Graphical Framework for OWL Ontologies), a visual standard for representing RDF/OWL knowledge structures, and designed with yEd software.

This graph translates the conceptual model of the project into a single visual structure, making explicit the relationships between people, works, events, and concepts related to Galileo Galilei.
