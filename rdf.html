A semantic representation of Galileo-related cultural heritage resources

Methodology

The RDF dataset describes a curated selection of ten cultural heritage items related to Galileo Galilei, such as books, people, audiovisual materials, historical events, and cultural artifacts. All metadata were structured and semantically enriched using Linked Open Data principles and ontologies such as schema.org, Dublin Coreand CIDOC-CRM.

Each item was initially described in a CSV file, containing structured metadata mapped to controlled vocabularies and authority records (e.g., VIAF, Wikidata). These CSV files were then transformed into RDF using Python scripts and the rdflib library.

Next, we moved on to the special case of the full-text TEI edition, transforming it into RDF as well.

From TEI to RDF

The part of text extracted from the Dialogo sopra i due massimi sistemi del mondo was encoded in TEI/XML following the TEI P5 guidelines. We then used a Python script with rdflib to parse the XML, extract entities (persons, schools of thought, locations) and structural divisions, and serialize everything as RDF/Turtle. The TEI file was analyzed to identify and extract key semantic elements. Named entities such as persons, places, and schools of thought were mapped using vocabularies such as schema.org and Dublin Core. Structural divisions like chapters and speakers turns were also represented as RDF classes and linked to their content.
